Connecting Shoppers, Bloggers, and Brands

Shoppers

Shoppers can save items they like from anywhere on the web, and as soon as those items go on sale, we'll ping them. It increases engagement as well as conversions.

Bloggers

Bloggers can import all of the items they're blogging about into one universal store front for their affiliate links. This info is one of the factors in defining their style-DNA.

Brands

With both shoppers and bloggers on our platform, this is the perfect resource for brands to tap into. They can find the right bloggers for their campaigns, and track very fine-grained results.

Who We Are

The Co-Founders

Lauren Jung & Atul Singh

We're Lauren and Atul and we're the co-founders of The Shelf. We met each other at a salsa dancing class in Princeton NJ about 2 and a half years ago and within days, decided that we were meant for each other (thank you Tom).

This very serendipitous meeting also happened to coincide with the demise of our day jobs. Micromanagers, monotony, and passionless projects eventually began to eat away at us. 6 months later we came up with the idea for The Shelf, and we both set to work (after happily quitting our day jobs). I handle the design/UX of our site as well as the front-end implementation. Atul has been in charge of the backend, integration, IP, and scalability.

Atul has a BTech from IIT Kharagpur and a PhD in CS from Rice University. His expertise is in software fault-tolerance and he has been published at top-tier conferences (NSDI, SOSP, Infocom). Most recently he worked at NEC Labs, Princeton, where he led a team in building the first all optical data center network architecture. I graduated from Furman University with a BA in Fine Art, and later completed a grad program in design from the Portfolio Center in Atlanta. I worked at an ad company for a brief period as well as a software components company. When I finished school, I co-founded a fabric design and pattern business (with my mom... because I'm cool). Our fabric and patterns are distributed worldwide, and we are sorta famous in quilting circles.
